FROM MAINFRAMES TO SMARTPHONES

Mainframes, the First Electronic Computers

Before the incoming of traditional computers, there were some mechanical machines used to execute simple calculations. One of the most famous machines is the Jacquar loom, which was able to create special designs in the clothes by reading a hole-punched cardboard. With the Electronic Age, vacuum tubes replaced the mechanical parts, thus allowing machines to make more calculations of various types. To set the type of the calculation they needed to regolate vacuum tubes with a welder. The first programmable electronic computer was Mark-1 Colossus, by Alan Turing. It was born to help British Army for decoding German codes in WII. Due to the secrecy of that project, the very first Programmable electronic computer was the ENIAC Computer. To imagine the greatness of this machine, just think that it was composed by five million hand-soldered connections and it was bigger than a room. One of the most important partecipants of this project was John Von Neumann. After WWII mainframes like ENIAC kept spreading all over the world and being increasingly powerful, thanks to the invention of transistors. Nowadays lots of companies use mainframes for specific purposes.


Personal Computers

From the end of 1960s, people made attempts to make computer extremely widespread to solve the problems of cost and size. In 1957 Olivetti, stimulated by Enrico Fermi, launched Eula 9300, one of the earliest ancestors of the modern computers. In 1970s Packard introduced new computers programmed with basic language, equipped with a small display, keybord and a printer. The real impulse for the proliferation of personal computer occurred thanks to the possibility of including several microprocessors in single integrated circuits. In 1981 was relased the first personal computer, he IBM 5150, known as IBM pc. It was expansive, had low power and needed expansive hard drives, but it was solid, reliable and expandible. IBM pc became the standard machine in the personal computer industry. On 24 January 1984, Apple introduced Macintosh. This computer was equipped with a set of grapical interfaces and a mouse. It was a business success, especially as to its operating system, MacOS, and the graphical user interface easily understandable. Later many of these feautures were "borrowed" by Microsoft in the creation of its Windows operating system. Moreover there was the spreading of the concept of "WIMP"(Windows, Icones, Menus and Pointer) that is now a standard of all interfaces.


Portable computers, Notebooks and Netbooks

In 70s the new personal computers couldn’t be brought with ease.
So, notebooks are one of the most important inventions of the ICT world.
The first portable computer made was the IBM 5100 and it was released in 1975. It weighted 25 kg.
After the IBM 5100 lighter and lighter portable computers were made, but it was difficult to go below 10 kg, because of the CRT (Cathode Ray Tube) monitor.
Few years later low-energy consumption and small-sized mass memories were invented.
The CRT monitors were substituted by light TFT (Thin Film Transistor) thanks to the invention of the transistors.
Finally, the first notebook was made: it was small-sized, slightly power-reduced and suitable for surfing, sending e-mails and word processing. Nowadays the are being replaced by tablets.
Notebooks are composed by two hardware elements of similar size connected together. Furthermore, it can be “closed” by superimposing the two blocks, so the notebook can be carried easily.
Notebook’s first costs were very high, because of the cost of producing TFT monitors. The cheapest were made with a monochrome screen. Nowadays there are no difference between desktop and notebook costs.


PDAs, Tablets and Smartphones

In the '80s, as PCs' evolution proceeded, the first
hand-held devices made their first apperarence. They
were called "PDAs", which stands for "Personal
Digital Assistant".
Theese devices were useful for managing contacts
and commitments, but they had more features like
the calculator, too. In few word, they were real
computers, which could stand in the palm of your
hand.
Over the years, PDAs were improved, so they were
able to syncronize your contacts and calendar,by
linking them with a cable to a PC, using infrared or
Bluetooth or even browsing the net. Moreover, their
hardware implementations (like better touchscreens or improved
CPUs) were fundamental and so
significative.
While the PDAs were furthermore evolving, in the
90s, another important device was evolving: the
mobile phone.
Theese devices were getting a lot of new features, the same that
PDAs had before. This parallel evolution brought to the birth of a
new, powerful device: the smartphone.
Smartphones included all the PDAs features,
but they had the possibility to make calls. This
technological evolution will mark the end of hand-
held computers.
In the new millennia the tactile display technology (Touchscreen)
has been implemented in other devices too, like notebooks
or desktop PCs. Notebooks got more compact, and
with their comfortable touchscreens a new device was introduced,
the tablet. There are a lot of type of
tablets, from the PCs-looking-like (for their hardware and
powerfulness) to "big-smartphones" (also known as
second-generation-tablets).
Theese devices are evolving in different manners, and they are
starting to take the place of laptops because of their compact and
light design, in fact in the last years some tablet
manufacturers equippd their products with thin keyboards,
which can be directly attached to the device,
making it look like a proper laptop.
The main feature that differentiates tablets and PCs
is the possibility to connect to the internet via cellular line
without external devices.
